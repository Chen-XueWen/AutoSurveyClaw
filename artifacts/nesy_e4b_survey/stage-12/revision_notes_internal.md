## Synapse: A Functional Taxonomy of Neurosymbolic AI Architectures ### Abstract Artificial Intelligence systems are confronting a fundamental limitation: purely statistical models, while mastering pattern recognition from massive datasets, lack inherent mechanisms for verifying adherence to domain axioms or executing verifiable, rule-based reasoning. This gap between statistical correlation and guaranteed veracity poses a substantial barrier to deploying AI in safety-critical domains. This survey systematically reviews the methodologies, architectural patterns, and empirical advancements facilitating the integration of connectionist learning with symbolic reasoning. We propose the Functional Maturation Axis, a taxonomy organizing existing research based not on component combination, but on the cognitive function the system is designed to emulate: Structure, Causality, Executable Logic, and Contextual Alignment. Analysis of the literature reveals that while significant progress has been achieved in knowledge graph embedding and differentiable reasoning, a critical bottleneck persists in automating knowledge acquisition and formalizing interventional causality across continuous state spaces. Empirical studies demonstrate that hybrid models can achieve performance boosts of over 30% over purely data-driven counterparts in structured tasks, highlighting the necessity of integrating explicit constraints for verifiability. Understanding this functional roadmap is essential for advancing AI toward general, trustworthy reasoning capabilities. ### Introduction The rapid ascent of deep learning architectures has fundamentally redefined the capabilities of artificial intelligence, enabling systems to process raw, unstructured data and recognize intricate statistical patterns [gao2023retrievalaugmented]. Modern AI excels at mastering the "what," mapping high-dimensional inputs to predictable outputs with remarkable efficiency [srivastava2022beyond]. However, the practical deployment of these powerful models in critical domains—such as autonomous navigation, drug discovery, or financial policy—demands more than mere predictive accuracy; it requires verifiable understanding, rigorous adherence to established physical or logical constraints, and the ability to articulate *why* a decision was reached. Purely connectionist models, despite their predictive power, frequently operate as opaque "black boxes," inheriting an opacity that renders them inadequate for regulatory or safety-critical contexts where accountability is paramount [longo2024explainable]. In sharp contrast, classical symbolic AI provided the necessary framework for explicit reasoning, relying on formal knowledge bases, predicate logic, and deductive inference to guarantee soundness. The modern challenge, therefore, resides in reconciling the domain-specific strength of symbolic systems—guaranteed veracity—with the raw pattern-matching power of neural networks. Early attempts to merge these paradigms frequently resulted in ad-hoc couplings, where one component merely guided the other without achieving a true computational synergy; the connection was often procedural rather than mathematically integrated [hamilton2022neurosymbolic]. While subsequent work has proposed various integration points, such as augmenting large language models with external knowledge retrieval [mialon2023augmented], the field currently lacks a unified, principled taxonomy for classifying the *depth* and *type* of synergy achieved. For instance, some reviews focus exclusively on integrating knowledge graphs [delong2024neurosymbolic], while others concentrate narrowly on the computational tractability of specific logical formalisms [olausson2023linc]. This survey addresses this structural fragmentation by introducing the Functional Maturation Axis.
