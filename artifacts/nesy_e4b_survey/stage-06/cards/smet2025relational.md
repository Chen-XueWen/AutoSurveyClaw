This paper introduces **Relational Neurosymbolic Markov Models (NeSy-MMs)**, presenting a novel class of end-to-end differentiable sequential models designed to address a critical tension in AI: the trade-off between the expressive power of deep learning and the necessity of formal, verifiable reasoning. The primary problem identified is that while state-of-the-art deep sequential models, such as transformers, excel in domains like Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Reinforcement Learning (RL), they inherently fail to guarantee the satisfaction of necessary logical constraints required for trustworthy deployment. While existing Neurosymbolic AI (NeSy) formalisms provide sound methods for enforcing these constraints, they suffer from exponential computational scaling when applied to long or complex sequential problems.

To overcome this scalability bottleneck, the authors propose NeSy-MMs. These models are specifically engineered to integrate and *provably* satisfy complex relational logical constraints within a differentiable, sequential framework. The technical contribution lies in developing a scalable strategy for both inference and learning within this constrained environment. This strategy reportedly combines approximate Bayesian inference techniques to maintain tractability across sequential data.

The core claim of the work is that NeSy-MMs successfully bridge the gap between deep probabilistic modeling and formal constraint satisfaction without incurring the prohibitive computational cost associated with previous NeSy approaches in sequential settings.
