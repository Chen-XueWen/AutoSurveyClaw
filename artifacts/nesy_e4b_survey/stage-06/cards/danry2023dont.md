# Don’t Just Tell Me, Ask Me: AI Systems that Intelligently Frame Explanations as Questions Improve Human Logical Discernment Accuracy over Causal AI explanations

*source: abstract_only | tokens: 147*

This work addresses the critical challenge of human reasoning fallibility, noting that cognitive biases and resource limitations degrade critical thinking skills, even when presented with information. The authors propose a novel intervention termed **AI-framed Questioning** as a mechanism to scaffold and actively engage users' reasoning processes. Instead of simply providing direct explanations or classifications, the system reformulates the information relevant to the AI's decision into targeted questions, compelling the user to actively participate in the logical discernment process.

The technical approach is fundamentally behavioral and cognitive, positioning the AI not as an oracle, but as an interactive Socratic guide. The study tested this method on a task requiring the discernment of logical validity within socially divisive statements. The key finding is a direct comparison of efficacy across three conditions: (1) no feedback, (2) standard causal AI explanations (from an always correct system), and (3) the proposed AI-framed Questioning. The results demonstrate that AI-framed Questioning significantly improved human logical discernment accuracy compared to both the baseline and the standard causal explanations.

While the specific quantitative metrics (e.g., percentage increase in accuracy) are truncated in the provided text, the core contribution is establishing the superiority of *interrogative scaffolding* over *declarative explanation* in promoting deeper critical engagement. This research builds upon existing work in explainable AI (XAI) by arguing that the *mode* of explanation—questioning versus stating—is a critical determinant of human uptake and reasoning improvement. The paper implicitly suggests that current causal AI explanations, while informative, are insufficient for maximizing human metacognitive effort, thereby identifying a gap in the current literature regarding the optimal interface design for cognitive scaffolding.
