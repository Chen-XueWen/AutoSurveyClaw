# Neurosymbolic AI for network intrusion detection systems: A survey

*source: abstract_only | tokens: 144*

This paper functions as a comprehensive **Survey / review paper** dedicated to mapping the integration of Neurosymbolic AI (NeSy AI) within Network Intrusion Detection Systems (NIDS). Its primary scope is to address the critical limitations inherent in purely data-driven approaches currently dominating the NIDS field.

The survey identifies the core deficiencies of conventional Deep Learning (DL) models, specifically citing issues related to high resource consumption, substantial computational demands, and, most critically, limited interpretability when analyzing complex network traffic. Furthermore, the authors highlight that these purely data-driven systems often struggle with detecting novel or rapidly evolving cyber threats—a key weakness in modern cybersecurity.

The central theme and proposed solution space revolve around the synergy of NeSy AI. The paper posits that by combining the advanced pattern recognition capabilities of neural processing (DL) with the structured, rule-based reasoning inherent to symbolic AI, NeSy AI can significantly enhance both the detection accuracy and the *interpretability* of complex network threats.

While the provided abstract does not detail the specific taxonomy, the structure suggests the survey will categorize existing NeSy AI architectures applied to NIDS based on *how* the symbolic and neural components are integrated (e.g., using symbolic knowledge graphs to constrain neural latent spaces, or using DL to extract features for symbolic rule engines). The key pattern observed across the surveyed work is the shift from "black-box" detection to "explainable" detection.

The identified gap, and the core contribution of the survey, is providing a structured overview of how this integration mitigates the interpretability crisis in NIDS. The paper aims to guide future research by synthesizing current methodologies, thereby establishing a clear roadmap for developing robust, explainable, and computationally efficient next-generation NIDS that can handle zero-day attacks. It positions itself as a necessary guide for researchers moving beyond purely empirical data modeling toward knowledge-infused AI systems in cybersecurity.
