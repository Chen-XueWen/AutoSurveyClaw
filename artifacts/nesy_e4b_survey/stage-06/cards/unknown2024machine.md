# MACHINE LEARNING STRATEGIES AND ALGORITHMS FOR ENHANCING REAL-TIME DATA PROCESSING IN DYNAMIC AND BIG DATA SYSTEMS

*source: abstract_only | tokens: 133*

This systematic review article meticulously surveys the landscape of machine learning strategies and algorithms tailored for enhancing real-time data processing within dynamic and big data ecosystems. The scope is broad, covering the application of standard ML paradigms—specifically supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning—to address the escalating demands for scalable and computationally efficient data pipelines. The authors structure their review by assessing the effectiveness of these models across various dynamic systems, establishing a comprehensive understanding of current state-of-the-art techniques for stream processing.

The key thematic pattern emerging from the literature is the trade-off between model complexity and real-time latency. While the review confirms the utility of deep learning architectures for feature extraction and pattern recognition in high-velocity data streams, it systematically identifies critical, persistent gaps in the field. These gaps represent significant areas for future research, particularly those related to data structure and knowledge integration. Foremost among the limitations are the challenges in handling highly heterogeneous data sources, the necessity for robust real-time adaptability in concept drift scenarios, and the critical need to embed privacy-preserving mechanisms directly into the processing framework.

The authors propose a conceptual framework for real-time machine learning, outlining necessary processes that move beyond mere algorithmic application toward holistic system design. From the perspective of Neurosymbolic AI, this review’s identified gaps are particularly salient. The reliance on pure ML models, as documented, struggles inherently with the interpretability and structured reasoning required when data sources are heterogeneous or when domain knowledge must constrain the model's output. The identified need for enhanced adaptability and structured handling of knowledge suggests a clear deficiency in the current literature regarding the integration of explicit symbolic knowledge graphs or logical constraints with the continuous, pattern-matching capabilities of neural networks.

This work serves as a valuable meta-analysis, providing a structured taxonomy of existing ML solutions. It complements other surveys by focusing less on specific algorithm comparisons (e.g., comparing CNNs vs. Transformers) and more on the *systemic architectural failures* when processing real-world, complex data streams. By pinpointing the limitations around heterogeneity and adaptability, the review implicitly frames the necessity for hybrid AI paradigms—such as those combining symbolic reasoning with neural representations—to achieve truly reliable and trustworthy real-time intelligence in big data environments.
