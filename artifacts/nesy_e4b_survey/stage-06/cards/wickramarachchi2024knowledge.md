# Knowledge Graphs of Driving Scenes to Empower the Emerging Capabilities of Neurosymbolic AI

*source: s2_fulltext | tokens: 3827*

This paper introduces **DSceneKG**, a comprehensive knowledge graph suite designed to serve as a realistic, multi-modal benchmark for evaluating the emerging capabilities of Neurosymbolic AI, particularly within the domain of autonomous driving. The authors identify a critical gap: while Neurosymbolic AI promises enhanced reliability and explainability by integrating explicit symbolic knowledge (like Knowledge Graphs) with deep learning, current evaluation benchmarks fail to capture the complexity, heterogeneity, and scale of real-world industrial data, unlike traditional benchmarks such as Freebase or WordNet.

To address this, DSceneKG is constructed by integrating raw, multimodal sensor data from multiple open autonomous driving datasets, including **PandaSet**, **NuScenes**, **Waymo Open Dataset**, and **KITTI**. The system formalizes this knowledge using the **Driving Scenes Ontology (DSO)**, developed in Web Ontology Language (OWL), which provides a dataset-agnostic semantic structure. The resulting knowledge graph instances are stored in **Resource Description Framework (RDF)** format, allowing for the explicit representation of relationships between objects and events across diverse driving scenarios.

DSceneKG's primary contribution is its utility across seven distinct Neurosymbolic tasks, demonstrating its capacity to support advanced reasoning beyond simple perception. For instance, in **Knowledge-based Entity Prediction (KEP)**, the authors cite prior work [6] achieving **0.87 Hits@1** by leveraging DSceneKG’s holistic scene representation to predict missing entities. Furthermore, the graph structure enables **Explainable Scene Clustering/Typing**, allowing for the generation of semantically rich, user-comprehensible cluster labels. For **Semantic Similarity**, a method [8] computes cosine similarity directly on DSceneKG embedding vectors, successfully identifying semantic parallels between visually dissimilar scenes.

The benchmark also supports advanced reasoning tasks, including **Knowledge Completion and Augmentation** (e.g., [9] using context-based labeling for unobserved entities), **Causality** (e.g., [11] enriching the graph for counterfactual reasoning), **Semantic Search** (via SPARQL queries or vector search over Knowledge Graph Embeddings), and **Cross-modal Retrieval** (e.g., [12] using DSceneKG embeddings to align text instructions with Bird's-Eye View features).

In summary, DSceneKG moves the evaluation frontier for Neurosymbolic AI from abstract, narrow benchmarks to a rich, domain-specific, and highly structured representation of real-world physical environments, thereby advancing the state-of-the-art in grounding and cognitive reasoning for autonomous systems.
