# Neurosymbolic AI in Cybersecurity: Bridging Pattern Recognition and Symbolic Reasoning

*source: abstract_only | tokens: 139*

This paper functions as a high-level survey and conceptual review, mapping the potential integration of Neurosymbolic AI (NSAI) specifically within the domain of Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS). Its primary scope is to argue for the necessity of moving beyond purely data-driven or purely rule-based security measures by synergistically combining the strengths of both paradigms. The central thesis is that NSAI offers a pathway to developing cybersecurity solutions that are simultaneously robust, adaptive, and highly interpretable.

The paper does not propose a novel architecture or present quantitative results on specific benchmarks; rather, it establishes a conceptual framework for the field. In terms of categorization, it frames the integration process as bridging "pattern recognition capabilities of neural networks" with the "explicit reasoning of symbolic systems." This suggests a thematic pattern of combining deep learning for feature extraction (the 'neuro' component) with formal logic or knowledge graphs for verification and reasoning (the 'symbolic' component).

The key themes explored revolve around enhancing the transparency and trustworthiness of IDS. Traditional neural approaches are noted for their pattern recognition power but suffer from the 'black box' problem, while symbolic systems are inherently interpretable but often lack the adaptability to novel, unseen threats. The paper posits that the integration mitigates these weaknesses, leading to more robust detection mechanisms.

Crucially, the review identifies significant, persistent challenges that define the current research gaps. These include ensuring model interpretability in complex, real-time threat environments, managing the substantial data requirements for training, and maintaining adaptability when the threat landscape undergoes rapid, non-linear changes. While the text does not cite specific datasets or benchmark metrics, its contribution lies in synthesizing these architectural and practical limitations, thereby guiding future research efforts toward addressing the difficult task of formalizing knowledge within dynamic, noisy network data. This review positions itself as a foundational overview, likely intended to guide subsequent, more specialized empirical studies.
