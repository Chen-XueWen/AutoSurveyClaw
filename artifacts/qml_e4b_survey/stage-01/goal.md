## 📚 Survey Design Blueprint: Exponential Quantum Advantage in Machine Learning

**Project ID:** `my-survey`
**Domain Focus:** Machine Learning $\cap$ Quantum Computing
**Quality Benchmark:** 4.0 (High Academic Rigor/Impact)

***

### 🧠 Topic Statement
This survey aims to systematically map, synthesize, and critically evaluate the theoretical frameworks and empirical evidence supporting the potential for **exponential quantum advantage** within specific, computationally intensive Machine Learning paradigms (e.g., kernel estimation, complex feature space mapping, optimization).

### 🗺️ Scope Definition
**In Scope (Included):**
1.  **Theoretical Models:** Papers proposing quantum circuits ($\text{QML}$ architectures) and formal complexity proofs demonstrating a super-polynomial or exponential speedup over the best-known classical counterparts for specific ML tasks.
2.  **Benchmark Tasks:** Focus areas include Quantum Support Vector Machines (QSVM), Quantum Neural Networks (QNNs) trained on structured data, and quantum optimization algorithms applied to feature selection.
3.  **Mathematical Rigor:** Works that provide mathematical derivations comparing the computational complexity ($O(N)$ scaling) of quantum vs. classical methods.

**Out of Scope (Excluded):**
1.  **General ML Surveys:** Broad reviews of deep learning or classical ML algorithms without a quantum comparison.
2.  **Pure Quantum Physics:** Papers focused solely on quantum hardware development, error correction, or quantum state preparation *without* a clearly defined ML application pathway.
3.  **NISQ-Only Implementations:** Empirical results relying exclusively on current Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) devices that do not yet claim or theoretically prove an advantage over classical simulation bounds. (Focus must remain on the *potential* advantage).
4.  **Low-Complexity Tasks:** Simple linear regression or basic classification tasks where quantum advantage is not theoretically established.

### 🎯 SMART Goal
**Specific:** To conduct a systematic review of peer-reviewed literature published over the last 5 years that provides rigorous theoretical or simulation-based evidence for an exponential quantum advantage in at least three distinct, complex Machine Learning tasks.
**Measurable:** The final survey manuscript must synthesize findings from a minimum of 30 highly relevant primary research articles, achieving an internal consensus score (based on structured synthesis) of $\ge 4.0$ across key areas (Theoretical Depth, Empirical Support, Integration Potential).
**Achievable:** By restricting the focus to "exponential advantage" and defining clear mathematical inclusion criteria, the scope is narrowed enough to be manageable for a dedicated systematic review effort.
**Relevant:** This addresses one of the most critical, high-impact, and rapidly evolving intersections in modern computational science, directly informing research direction for industry and academia.
**Time-bound:** The comprehensive draft survey must be completed and internally vetted within **six months** of the commencement of the literature search phase.

### 📜 Inclusion Criteria
*   **Publication Type:** Must be published in top-tier, peer-reviewed journals (e.g., Nature Quantum Information, IEEE Transactions on Quantum Engineering) or major, peer-reviewed conference proceedings (e.g., NeurIPS, ICML, QIP).
*   **Relevance Threshold:** The paper must explicitly address the *gap* between classical computational limits and quantum computational capabilities for an ML objective function.
*   **Recency Requirement:** Primary focus must be on publications within the last **5 years (2019–Present)** to capture the rapid evolution of QML theory.

### 🚫 Exclusion Criteria
*   **Non-Peer-Reviewed:** Preprints (e.g., arXiv) without subsequent formal publication or peer review confirmation.
*   **Methodology:** Papers that only claim "potential" without providing either a mathematical proof of complexity advantage or a detailed theoretical roadmap to achieving that advantage.
*   **Scope Creep:** Any paper that discusses quantum computation in general terms without applying it specifically to a recognized ML loss function or optimization problem.

### ⚙️ Constraints & Metrics
*   **Target Paper Count:** $25 - 40$ core primary research articles.
*   **Quality Threshold:** $\ge 4.0$ (This mandates that the synthesis must move beyond mere summarization to *critical evaluation* and *gap identification*).

### ✅ Success Criteria
A high-quality survey will not merely list papers; it will achieve the following:
1.  **Taxonomy Development:** Develop a clear, multi-dimensional taxonomy classifying existing QML approaches based on the *type* of quantum advantage claimed (e.g., exponential vs. polynomial vs. quadratic).
2.  **Roadmap Identification:** Synthesize a consensus-driven, actionable roadmap identifying the necessary theoretical breakthroughs (e.g., fault tolerance requirements, specific quantum primitives) required to move from current simulation-based results to proven exponential advantage.
3.  **Gap Analysis:** Clearly delineate the major unresolved research gaps, particularly in the necessary data encoding and measurement techniques required for practical adoption.
