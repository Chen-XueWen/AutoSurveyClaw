# ECA-Net: Efficient Channel Attention for Deep Convolutional Neural Networks

*source: s2_fulltext | tokens: 11210*

Wang et al. (2019) introduce the Efficient Channel Attention (ECA) module to resolve the complexity-performance paradox in deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs). While channel attention mechanisms like SENet demonstrate significant accuracy gains, they typically increase model complexity through dimensionality reduction in fully connected layers. The authors argue that this reduction is unnecessary and detrimental to learning effective channel dependencies. Consequently, ECA-Net proposes a lightweight alternative that eliminates dimensionality reduction entirely. Instead of using two FC layers, the ECA module employs a 1D convolution to capture local cross-channel interaction directly on the channel dimension following global average pooling. Crucially, the kernel size $k$ of this 1D convolution is adaptively determined by the channel dimension $C$ via a non-linear mapping ($k = \lfloor \frac{\log_2(C)}{2} + 0.5 \rceil_{\text{odd}}$), ensuring the coverage of interaction scales appropriately without manual tuning.

Extensive evaluations on ImageNet-1K and MS COCO validate the module's efficiency and effectiveness. On ImageNet classification using ResNet-50 as a backbone, ECA-Net achieves a Top-1 accuracy of 77.48%, surpassing the vanilla ResNet-50 (75.20%) and the SENet baseline (76.71%), while maintaining identical model complexity (24.37M parameters, 3.86 GFLOPs). This represents a 2.28% absolute improvement over the baseline with zero additional computational cost. Ablation studies confirm that avoiding dimensionality reduction is critical, as variants without FC layers outperform the original SE block. The module generalizes well to object detection and instance segmentation tasks on MS COCO using Faster R-CNN, Mask R-CNN, and RetinaNet. For instance, on ResNet-50 backbones, ECA improves AP by 0.3% over SENet in Faster R-CNN while retaining the original parameter count. Compared to other attention modules like CBAM and A2-Nets, ECA-Net achieves competitive or superior accuracy with significantly lower FLOPs and parameter overhead. For lightweight architectures like MobileNetV2, ECA-Net also yields a 0.9% Top-1 accuracy gain. Visualization of learned weights shows ECA modules produce more class-specific weights compared to SE blocks, particularly in deeper stages, indicating better discriminative ability. The authors conclude that avoiding dimensionality reduction and utilizing local cross-channel interaction via 1D convolution provides a superior trade-off, making ECA-Net a highly efficient, plug-and-play component for modern CNN architectures. This efficiency is particularly relevant for resource-constrained environments, offering a pathway to integrate attention mechanisms without the traditional computational penalty.
