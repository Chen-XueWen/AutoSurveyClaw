# A Multi-Modal Distributed Real-Time IoT System for Urban Traffic Control (Invited Paper)

Addressing the critical issue of urban traffic congestion and associated fuel wastage, this invited paper proposes a distributed IoT framework designed to supersede legacy Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) systems that rely on inefficient round-robin scheduling. While recent IoT-based frameworks attempt to control traffic movement using lane-specific density metrics, they frequently suffer from low accuracy, primarily attributed to a lack of comprehensive emergency vehicle image datasets required to robustly train deep neural networks (DNNs). To resolve this, the authors introduce a novel distributed architecture underpinned by a two-stage vehicle detector. This approach exploits the empirical observation that major structural changes to road infrastructure are rare, allowing the detection model to maintain stability without constant retraining.

The system demonstrates a vehicle detection accuracy of 77% on the UA-DETRAC dataset, a standard benchmark for urban traffic monitoring. This result addresses the specific gap in handling diverse vehicle classifications, including emergency vehicles, which previous density-based models struggled to identify accurately. By distributing processing across the IoT network, the framework aims to facilitate real-time control, contrasting with the latency inherent in centralized PLC logic.
