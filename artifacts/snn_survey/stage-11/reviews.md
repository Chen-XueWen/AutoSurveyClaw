# Peer Review: Set Neural Networks: A Comprehensive Survey on Architectures and Theoretical Foundations

## Reviewer A (Survey Methodology Expert)
**Recommendation: Major Revision**

This survey attempts to address a fragmented landscape of Set Neural Networks (SNNs) with a unified taxonomy. However, the execution falls significantly short of the standards required for a top-tier venue survey (e.g., NeurIPS/ICML/Survey Journal).

### Strengths
1.  **Taxonomy Structure:** The proposed five-pillar taxonomy (Aggregation, Relational, Implicit, Generative, Efficiency) is conceptually sound and attempts to bridge discrete and continuous representations. It provides a logical framework for organizing the literature.
2.  **Comparative Tables:** The inclusion of comparison tables (Tables 1-5) is a strong feature that aids in understanding method differences across benchmarks.
3.  **Systematic Literature Review (SLR):** The paper attempts to describe an SLR protocol (Section 5), outlining search strings and inclusion criteria, which is essential for survey rigor.

### Weaknesses
1.  **Citation Recency and Volume:** The survey fails the quantitative benchmarks for a top-tier survey. The citation count appears to be around 19-20 papers in the tables, whereas the standard requirement is 30-60+ foundational and recent works. Furthermore, the citation recency is critically low; the automated check indicates only 5% of cited works are from the last 3 years (target: >=30%). A survey on "Set Neural Networks" published in 2024/2025 must heavily weigh 2023-2024 literature (e.g., Diffusion, recent GNNs).
2.  **Structural Integrity:** The manuscript contains a major structural error where the core content is duplicated and scrambled. Sections 1-7 appear, followed by a second sequence starting with Section 5 (Methodology) through Section 11. Section 5 (Methodology) is placed *after* Section 7 (Conclusion) in the first sequence, and then re-introduced later. This suggests a copy-paste error that renders the document unreadable in its current state.
3.  **Methodology Section Placement:** The SLR methodology section is currently located after the Conclusion in the first half of the text, and then repeated. This violates standard survey organization where the methodology should precede the main review to establish credibility.

### Actionable Revisions
1.  **Fix Document Structure:** Remove the duplicated sections (Sections 5-11 in the second half). Ensure the logical flow is: Introduction -> Related Work -> Methodology -> Taxonomy -> Detailed Review -> Comparative Analysis -> Challenges -> Conclusion.
2.  **Expand Citations:** Significantly increase the bibliography. You need at least 30-60 distinct citations. You must incorporate more recent works from 2023-2024 to meet the recency threshold.
3.  **Update Methodology:** Move the Methodology section to immediately follow Related Work. Ensure the search dates and inclusion criteria are clearly defined to justify the selected corpus.
4.  **Verify Citations:** Check the "Batch Norm [ioffe2024batch]" citation. Batch Normalization was published in 2015. A 2024 citation is likely a hallucination or error.

## Reviewer B (Domain Expert)
**Recommendation: Major Revision**

While the high-level goal of unifying graph, implicit, and generative set models is valuable, the technical depth and accuracy of the analysis require significant improvement. There are factual errors in the citations and some technical misclassifications that would mislead readers in the field.

### Strengths
1.  **Theoretical Grounding:** The discussion of the Weisfeiler-Lehman (WL) expressivity bottleneck is accurate and appropriately contextualized across the different pillars.
2.  **Unified Perspective:** The attempt to link implicit fields (NeRF) with set processing (point clouds) is a valuable insight that is often overlooked in siloed surveys.

### Weaknesses
1.  **Factual Errors & Hallucinations:**
    *   **Batch Norm Date:** The survey cites `[ioffe2024batch]` for Batch Normalization. Ioffe and Szegedy's paper is from 2015 (ICML 2015). Citing a 2024 arXiv version as the source is factually incorrect and damages credibility.
    *   **ControlNet Classification:** ControlNet is classified under "Implicit & Coordinate-Based Representations" in Table 1 and Table 4. ControlNet is a conditional control mechanism for diffusion models (image generation), not a set representation method per se. This conflates the *application* of a set model with the *methodology* of set representation.
    *   **"Helvetica Scenario":** The abstract mentions the "Helvetica scenario" of mode collapse. This is not standard terminology in the field; the standard term is simply "mode collapse." This suggests the text was generated by an AI with hallucinated or overly obscure terminology.
2.  **Method Misclassification:**
    *   **ECA-Net / GhostNet:** These are listed under "Efficiency" and "Set Neural Networks." While they are efficient, they are primarily CNN architectures (channel attention, ghost modules). Their inclusion as core "Set Neural Networks" is tenuous unless the survey explicitly argues for their adaptation to set layers, which is not clearly demonstrated beyond claiming "adapted to set architectures."
    *   **Deep Sets vs. GNNs:** The distinction between Deep Sets and GNNs is drawn as "Invariance vs. Equivariance." While true, the survey glosses over the fact that modern GNNs (like GraphSAGE) often use invariance for the graph-level task. The boundary is not as clean as the taxonomy suggests.
3.  **Depth of Analysis:** The "Detailed Review" sections often re-summarize abstracts rather than critically analyzing implementation details. For instance, the discussion on implicit fields mentions "multiple forward passes" as a cost but does not discuss specific acceleration techniques like octrees or hash tables (e.g., Instant-NGP, 2023).

### Actionable Revisions
1.  **Correct Citations:** Fix the BatchNorm citation to 2015. Verify all other dates and venues.
2.  **Refine Taxonomy:** Remove CNN-specific efficiency methods (ECA-Net, GhostNet) unless explicitly framed as "Efficiency for Set Layers." Move ControlNet to a Generative section or remove it if it doesn't fit the SNN definition.
3.  **Deepen Technical Analysis:** Replace generic summaries with specific architectural bottlenecks. For implicit fields, discuss NeRF acceleration techniques (sparse voxels, hash encoding) rather than just "high computational cost."
4.  **Remove Hallucinated Terms:** Replace "Helvetica scenario" with "mode collapse." Ensure all terminology is standard in the ML community.

## Reviewer C (Writing and Rigor Expert)
**Recommendation: Major Revision**

The writing quality is hindered by severe structural issues and signs of automated generation. The paper requires a complete rewrite of the organization and significant editing to remove generic phrasing and improve academic tone.

### Strengths
1.  **Prose Flow:** When the text is coherent (e.g., the Introduction), the sentences are generally well-constructed and follow academic conventions.
2.  **Abstract:** The abstract is informative, summarizing the contributions and findings clearly.

### Weaknesses
1.  **Structural Duplication:** The manuscript contains a massive error where Sections 5-11 repeat the content of Sections 5-9 but with different numbering and placed *after* the Conclusion. This indicates a "placeholder" style error where the author pasted a template twice.
    *   *Example:* Section 5 (Comparative Analysis) appears after Introduction. Then Section 5 (Methodology) appears after Conclusion.
    *   *Impact:* The paper is effectively 50% redundant and currently unusable.
2.  **AI Boilerplate Detection:** The text contains multiple phrases typical of LLM generation, which reduces the scholarly voice. Identified instances include:
    *   "comprehensive overview" (Abstract, Section 2)
    *   "bridge the gap" (Abstract, Section 11)
    *   "cutting-edge applications" (Section 1)
    *   "unified framework" (Section 1, Section 2)
    *   "critical insights" (Abstract, Section 1)
    *   "significantly enhances" (Section 4)
    *   "conducive to" (Section 6 - implied in style)
    *   "concerted effort" (Section 9)
3.  **Hedging and Vagueness:** There is excessive use of hedging language that weakens claims.
    *   "suggesting that scalable alternatives exist"
    *   "likely define the next generation"
    *   "may not generalize"
    *   This reduces the authority of the survey's conclusions.
4.  **Formatting:** The tables are inconsistent. Table 1 lists "Deep Sets" with venue "NIPS 2017*" while Table 8.1 lists "Deep Sets" with Year "2017". The formatting of years and venues is not uniform.

### Actionable Revisions
1.  **Fix Duplication:** Cut the entire second sequence of sections (starting from the second "5. Methodology") and ensure the sections are numbered sequentially 1-7 (or 1-10 with Limitations).
2.  **Remove Boilerplate:** Replace generic phrases like "comprehensive overview" with specific descriptions (e.g., "systematic review of X"). Replace "bridge the gap" with "unifies the literature."
3.  **Strengthen Claims:** Reduce hedging. Where possible, state findings directly. Instead of "suggesting that scalable alternatives exist," write "indicating that scalable alternatives are viable."
4.  **Standardize Tables:** Ensure all tables follow the same citation format (Author, Year, Venue) and that all metrics are attributed to their original sources without ambiguity.
5.  **Check for Placeholders:** Ensure no section reads like a template. The current duplication suggests the author used a "Section 5" placeholder that was not cleared.
